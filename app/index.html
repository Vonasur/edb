It is human nature to struggle on and try to problem solve on our own. Dogs (like many things) are much more complicated that we realise: at any given time their behaviour is likely being influenced by their breed, breeding, genetics, biology, health, gender, mood, personality, past experiences and the current situation and environment. It is important that we spend time teaching and guiding our dogs so that they know what is expected of them, and this is where the role of a professional dog trainer can be of assistance to a dog owner – we can use our experience to advise you how best to approach training for the specific outcomes you are looking to achieve.

Check out our dog and puppy training services here. Sometimes, things can go a bit wrong for dogs, through no fault of the owner or individual dog. Perhaps a traumatic event occurs, like an illness or a fight, or perhaps early socialisation didn’t go so well. In these circumstances, dog may start to show inappropriate behaviours such as barking, growling, snapping, biting, destructive behaviour or house soiling.

This is when hiring an appropriately qualified behaviourist will be most useful to you, as they will be able to pull together all of the different factors contributing to your dog’s behaviour, and work with you to help change the dog’s feelings and behaviour to something more positive and appropriate.
